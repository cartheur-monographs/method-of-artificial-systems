# The Method of Artificial Systems

`The Method of Artificial Systems` is a long-running manuscript on artificial life, robotics, and machine intelligence. Its central claim is that synthetic entities should be designed and studied as autonomous systems in their own right, not merely as partial imitations of human intelligence.

The work is written in the spirit of a manifesto, but its aim is constructive. It proposes a vocabulary and method for thinking about embodied machine life through:

- context and environmental coupling
- feature formation
- orchestration across physical and abstract components
- bounded choice
- autonomy as an engineered property

At its core, the project asks:

`What is artificial life, if we begin from the machine as a situated system rather than from intelligence as imitation?`

## Background

The project was first conceived in 1987 and later developed into an M.Phil thesis in 2008. This repository preserves that history while also serving as an active revision space for a more focused and contemporary articulation of the argument.

Some parts of the manuscript are archival in tone and reflect the moment in which they were first written. Others are current revision materials intended to clarify the work as a modern statement on embodied AI and artificial life.

## Repository Layout

- `source/`: manuscript sources, figures, and export formats
- `output/`: compiled document outputs
- `literature/`: reference archive and supporting materials
- `thinkers/`: historical concept documents and related notes
- `revision/`: current editorial workspace for restructuring and rewriting the manuscript

## Relation to Sibling Works

This repository sits alongside other monograph-scale projects by the same author in the parent `monographs/` directory. The cleanest way to read them together is as one research program with different levels of focus.

- `method-of-artificial-systems`: the conceptual anchor, defining the ontology, vocabulary, and method for artificial life and embodied machine intelligence
- `symbolic-logic`: the historical and conceptual lineage from symbolic logic into machine organization, control, and early embodied intelligence
- `behavior-hybrid-substrate`: the executable architecture and distributed-substrate implementation track
- `aibo-rcode`: concrete robotic behavior examples and code-level case studies

In that larger context, `The Method of Artificial Systems` should be read as the broad methodological statement that the sibling works support, extend, or instantiate.
