# Proposed Structure

This structure keeps the spirit of the manuscript but makes the argument easier to follow.

## 1. Preface on the history of the project

Purpose:
Explain that the work has a long development history and that some ideas originated in earlier technical and cultural contexts.

## 2. Introduction

Purpose:
State the problem, the failure mode in prevailing AI framings, and the alternative proposed here.

Core questions:

- What is an artificial system?
- Why is this a better starting point than conventional AI language?
- What kinds of machines become thinkable under this framework?

## 3. Limits of dominant AI approaches

Purpose:
Critique existing framings without making critique the center of the manuscript.

Possible material from current draft:

- The reluctance of AI
- parts of the literature survey
- portions of the simple conclusion

## 4. Conceptual foundations

Purpose:
Define the minimum vocabulary needed to understand the method.

Suggested order:

- system
- environment
- entity
- feature
- orchestration
- choice
- controller
- autonomy

## 5. The method of artificial systems

Purpose:
Present the positive framework clearly and in one place.

This should become the intellectual center of the manuscript.

Suggested subsections:

- Method of expression
- The choice methodology
- Constraints, states, and discretionary limits
- Orchestration as an organizing principle

## 6. Embodiment and experiment

Purpose:
Show how the theory becomes testable in physical or simulated systems.

Possible material from current draft:

- Testing choice
- Featured power
- Robotic software abstractions
- Wireless power system material

## 7. Results and interpretive discussion

Purpose:
Explain what the experiments do and do not show.

Important caution:
Do not let technical description stand in for argument. Tie each result back to the theory of choice and orchestration.

## 8. Implications

Purpose:
Extend the framework outward.

Possible topics:

- artificial life
- robotics
- machine consciousness
- ethics and governance
- design methodology

## 9. Conclusion

Purpose:
Restate the contribution in compact form and name the next stage of work.

## 10. Appendices

Purpose:
Preserve historically important auxiliary material without overloading the main narrative.

Likely candidates:

- Replicas--The Plan
- additional conceptual sketches
- archival notes that matter but interrupt flow
